Chocolate Chip Cookies\nIngredients:\n1 cup butter, softened\n1 cup white sugar\n1 cup packed brown sugar\n2 eggs\n2 teaspoons vanilla extract\n3 cups all-purpose flour\n1 teaspoon baking soda\n2 teaspoons hot water\n1/2 teaspoon salt\n2 cups semisweet chocolate chips\n1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)\nInstructions:\nPreheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).\nCream together the butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.\nBeat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.\nDissolve baking soda in hot water. Add to batter along with salt.\nStir in flour, chocolate chips, and nuts.\nDrop by large spoonfuls onto ungreased pans.\nBake for about 10 minutes in the preheated oven, or until edges are nicely browned.\nCool on wire racks.\n2. Oatmeal Raisin Cookies\nIngredients:\n1 cup butter, softened\n1 cup packed brown sugar\n1/2 cup white sugar\n2 eggs\n1 teaspoon vanilla extract\n1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour\n1 teaspoon baking soda\n1 teaspoon cinnamon\n1/2 teaspoon salt\n3 cups rolled oats\n1 cup raisins\nInstructions:\nPreheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).\nCream together butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until smooth.\nBeat in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla.\nCombine flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt; stir into the creamed mixture.\nStir in oats and raisins.\nDrop by rounded tablespoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets.\nBake 10 to 12 minutes until light and golden. Do not overbake.\nCool slightly, then remove from cookie sheets to cool completely.\n3. Peanut Butter Cookies\nIngredients:\n1/2 cup butter, softened\n1/2 cup white sugar\n1/2 cup packed brown sugar\n1/2 cup peanut butter\n1 egg\n1 teaspoon vanilla extract\n3/4 cup all-purpose flour\n1/2 teaspoon baking soda\n1/4 teaspoon salt\n1/2 cup rolled oats (optional)\nInstructions:\nPreheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).\nCream together butter, white sugar, brown sugar, and peanut butter until smooth.\nBeat in egg and vanilla.\nCombine flour, baking soda, and salt; stir into the peanut butter mixture.\nMix in oats if desired.\nDrop by tablespoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets.\nBake for 8 to 10 minutes in the preheated oven. Allow cookies to cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.\n4. Sugar Cookies\nIngredients:\n1 1/2 cups butter, softened\n2 cups white sugar\n4 eggs\n1 teaspoon vanilla extract\n5 cups all-purpose flour\n2 teaspoons baking powder\n1 teaspoon salt\nInstructions:\nCream together butter and sugar until smooth.\nBeat in eggs and vanilla.\nStir in flour, baking powder, and salt.\nCover, and chill dough for at least one hour (or overnight).\nPreheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).\nRoll out dough on floured surface 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.\nCut into shapes with any cookie cutter. Place cookies 1 inch apart on ungreased cookie sheets.\nBake 6 to 8 minutes in preheated oven. Cool completely.\n5. Snickerdoodle Cookies\nIngredients:\n1/2 cup butter, softened\n1/2 cup shortening\n1 1/2 cups white sugar\n2 eggs\n2 teaspoons vanilla extract\n2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour\n2 teaspoons cream of tartar\n1 teaspoon baking soda\n1/4 teaspoon salt\n2 tablespoons white sugar\n2 teaspoons ground cinnamon\nInstructions:\nPreheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).\nCream together butter, shortening, 1 1/2 cups sugar, the eggs and the vanilla.\nBlend in the flour, cream of tartar, soda and salt.\nShape dough by rounded spoonfuls into balls.\nMix the 2 tablespoons sugar and the cinnamon.\nRoll balls of dough in mixture.\nPlace 2 inches apart on ungreased baking sheets.\nBake 8 to 10 minutes, or until set but not too hard.\nRemove immediately from baking sheets.
